Editing a City Page

Each city has its own services page with a customized hero, intro paragraph, and section headings. This guide walks you through editing all of them.

[SCREENSHOT: A city services page on the website showing the hero, intro, and services list]

Where to Find It

Section titled “Where to Find It”

English city pages:

  1. Log in to Directus.
  2. Click Content in the left sidebar.
  3. Click services_page.
  4. You’ll see a list of cities. Click the city you want to edit.

Spanish city pages:

  • Same steps, but click services_page_es instead.

[SCREENSHOT: The services_page collection showing a list of city entries]

Field Reference

Section titled “Field Reference”
FieldWhat it does
cityThe URL slug for this city (e.g., eagle-pass) — do not change this
hero_titleThe headline shown in the hero banner for this city
hero_descriptionThe short paragraph in the hero
hero_imgThe background image for the hero banner
intro_bodyThe intro paragraph below the hero — supports markdown formatting
services_titleThe heading above the services list (e.g., “Our Services in Eagle Pass”)
local_titleThe heading above the Local Considerations section
local_listThe local consideration items — linked from the local_list collection

Step-by-Step: Editing a City Page

Section titled “Step-by-Step: Editing a City Page”
  1. Open services_page and click the city you want to edit.
  2. Update the hero_title — type the city-specific headline.
  3. Update the hero_description — type the short hero paragraph.
  4. To change the hero background image, click hero_img and upload a new photo.
  5. Click the intro_body field and update the intro paragraph.
  6. Update services_title with the heading for the services list.
  7. Update local_title with the heading for the local section.
  8. Click Save when done.

Formatting the Intro Body

Section titled “Formatting the Intro Body”

The intro_body field supports markdown. You can use bold text, bullet points, and line breaks to make the content easier to read.

For the full markdown reference, see the Formatting Guide.

The City Slug Field

Section titled “The City Slug Field”

Caution

The city field contains the URL slug — the short name used in the website address. For example, eagle-pass makes the page live at yoursite.com/services/eagle-pass.

Never change the city slug. Changing it breaks the page’s URL and removes it from Google search results.

Editing Local Considerations Items

Section titled “Editing Local Considerations Items”

The items in the Local Considerations section come from the local_list collection. To add, edit, or remove items, see your developer or follow these steps:

  1. Click Content → local_list.
  2. Click the item you want to edit.
  3. Update the title and description in the Translations section.
  4. Click Save.

Changes to local_list items will appear on any city page that uses them.
